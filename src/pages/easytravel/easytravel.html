EASY TRAVEL GUIDE

TRANSPORTATION

Gett Taxi: Gett Taxi is changing how people move around their city. Gett is the #1 taxi service in Europe. It is an application that you can download for free. You have the choice to pay either by credit card, with your information added, or in cash.

Tel-O-Fun Bike Rental: Tel Aviv offers a bike sharing rental system which is a great way to explore the city. Tel-O-Fun has 2,000 bicycles, 200 stations, and 130 kilometers of riding lanes. The bikes are available 24/7 at a minimal fee.

FINANCIAL GUIDANCE

Change Machines: It is possible to obtain cash in the New Israeli Shekel (NIS) currency from the airport upon arrival. The change machine dispenses shekels at an exchange rate. Your bank will charge you a transaction fee.

Change Stores: Change stores are located throughout the country. It is a simple way to exchange money.

Tax Refund: When making a purchase that exceeds 400 NIS, you are entitled to a VAT refund. This applies if the store is registered with the Ministry of Tourism. Stores that are registered will have a sign in their store noting this. To receive your VAT refund, you must fill in a form that the store provides you with, get a receipt, and show the item(s) that you have purchased. The refund is given at the airport upon departure. Ensure you leave sufficient time at the airport to receive your refund.

Tipping :In Israel, it is customary to tip hotel personnel, restaurant staff, and baggage handlers. The average tip is between 12-15%.
